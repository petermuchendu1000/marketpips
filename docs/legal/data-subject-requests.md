# Data-Subject Requests (DSR) Process

> **Launch template — pending legal review.** How users exercise their data
> rights and how we handle those requests operationally.

_Last updated: July 2026_

## Rights users can exercise
Access · Rectification · Erasure · Portability · Restriction · Objection ·
Withdraw consent. (Scope subject to applicable law and legal-retention holds.)

## How to submit
Via the [Help](/help) page or by emailing support with the account email. We may
verify identity before actioning a request (to protect the account).

## Handling workflow (internal)
1. **Log** the request (type, requester, date) in the DSR register.
2. **Verify** the requester's identity.
3. **Locate** data across: `profiles`, wallets/transactions/orders/positions,
   KYC storage, notifications, audit logs, and backups.
4. **Assess** legal holds (AML/financial retention may block erasure — restrict
   instead).
5. **Action** (export / correct / delete / anonymise / restrict).
6. **Respond** within the statutory timeframe (e.g. commonly ~30 days; confirm
   per jurisdiction) with what was done.
7. **Record** completion for accountability.

## SLAs & escalation
- Acknowledge promptly; complete within statutory window.
- Complex/voluminous requests may be extended with notice.
- Disputes escalate to the privacy owner; users may complain to the relevant
  data-protection authority.
